Feminist Theory and Closure Have Close Relations

The nature of Chaucer’s tales can be directly related to the feminist theory. The feminist theory characterizes one’s writings by the masculine and feminine aspects included. Grudin writes, the feminine structure of a work includes a beginning, middle and an end. The masculine structure includes ending a work in disarray. Grudin includes a quote from Caroyln Dinshaw’s reading of Troilus and Criseyde,“ ‘like a man... resolves or occludes contradictions and disorder, fulfills the need for wholeness” (51). This statement provides a clear correlation between the masculine perspective of writing and Chaucer’s evasion of a conclusion.
